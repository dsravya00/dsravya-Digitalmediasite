Exercise 1

This is my first sketch in p5.js. As a beginner, it helped me learn to create loops with shapes like rectangles, ellipses, and triangles while adding rotation. The goal was to display these shapes and let viewers interact by moving the mouse. I enjoyed how the final design created a vibrant rainbow color effect. I liked using mouseX and mouseY to create random elements, and I’m satisfied with how it turned out. It reflects my idea of presenting a colorful sketch that combines aesthetics and coding. I also noticed the differences between drawing and coding when illustrating design ideas.

Excercise 2

In Exercise #2, I used emojis to create an interactive sketch with keyboard keys and added text. I applied built-in functions like mouseIsPressed and keyIsPressed, using shortcuts like ++ and --. My goal was to make shapes move like an arch, resembling an open and close mouth, while also playing with images. I focused on rotating the shapes because I enjoy seeing movement in digital art.
